I am currently a **PhD candidate in Computer Science at Rutgers University**, advised by **Dr. Vladimir Pavlovic**. My recent work focuses on improving the general-purpose capabilities and safety of **LLMs / MLLMs**, with particular interests in **hallucination mitigation**, **visual reasoning**, and **post-training methods** such as **RL** and **SFT**.

Before Rutgers, I earned an **M.S. in Informatics** from **Pennsylvania State University** and a **B.Eng. in Electronic Information Engineering** from the **University of Electronic Science and Technology of China**.

I have also worked as an **Applied Scientist II Intern at Amazon**, where I worked on large-scale retrieval models and customer representation learning for recommendation systems.

For recruiters, this page is meant to give a quick read on my research taste, internship experience, and technical strengths. For academic collaborators, it highlights my publications, current directions, and teaching and service work.

## Research snapshot

- LLM / MLLM capability improvement and post-training
- Object hallucination detection and mitigation in LVLMs
- Visual reasoning and mechanistic interpretability for vision-language models
- Earlier work on federated, multimodal, and semi-supervised learning
